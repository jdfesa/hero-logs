# Product Vision

HeroLogs is a private life tracking app for Android.

The product should help users understand how their real days are unfolding without forcing them to manually journal every activity. The app observes permitted on-device signals, reconstructs a daily timeline, and translates that timeline into simple wellbeing dimensions.

## One Sentence

HeroLogs turns your day into a private, explainable life log that stays on your phone.

## Core Idea

The app should build a daily map of:

- Where the user went.
- How long they stayed.
- How they moved between places.
- Which routines, social moments, rest periods, and productive blocks appeared.
- How those patterns affected simple life scores.

The experience should feel quiet, useful, and trustworthy. It should not feel like surveillance, productivity pressure, or a social network.

## Target Experience

The user installs HeroLogs, grants permissions intentionally, lives their normal day, and opens the app later to see a reconstructed timeline and a score summary.

The app should answer:

- What happened today?
- Where did my time go?
- Why did my score move?
- What repeated patterns are appearing?
- What small correction would make the timeline more accurate?

## Product Pillars

### Private By Design

The MVP should avoid accounts, cloud sync, and server-side processing. Sensitive data should stay on the device.

### Automatic First

Manual input should improve the app, not be required for the app to be useful.

### Explainable Scoring

Scores must be traceable to understandable signals. A user should be able to inspect why a score increased or decreased.

### Correctable Memory

HeroLogs should admit uncertainty. Unknown places, unclear movement, and mistaken classifications should be editable.

### Long-Term Perspective

Daily scores matter, but trends matter more. The product should help users see their life over weeks and months.

## Non-Goals For MVP

- Social features.
- Public profiles.
- Cloud dashboards.
- Cross-device sync.
- AI coaching as a primary feature.
- Medical diagnosis or clinical health advice.
- A full replacement for Google Maps Timeline, Google Fit, or a habit tracker.

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when a user can:

- Complete onboarding and understand what data is collected.
- See today's reconstructed timeline.
- Correct an unknown place or misclassified trip.
- View a daily LifeBar with clear dimensions.
- Understand the reason behind each score.
- Open a weekly recap.
- Trust that data remains local.
